
\subsection{MedCalc-Bench}

\noindent\textbf{Description:} \\
\noindent MedCalc-Bench is a benchmark designed to evaluate models on their ability to compute clinically relevant values from patient notes. Each instance consists of a clinical note describing the patient's condition, a diagnostic question targeting a specific medical value, and a ground truth response.

\vspace{1em}

\noindent\textbf{Category:} Clinical Decision Support \\
\noindent\textbf{Subcategory:} Supporting Diagnostic Decisions

\vspace{1em}

\noindent\textbf{Context:} \\
\noindent The context provides the clinical background needed to answer a diagnostic or prognostic question. It typically includes a progress note summarizing the patient's condition and relevant clinical events.

\begin{examplebox}
\noindent\textit{Example:} \\
\noindent\texttt{Patient note: A 70-year-old female was rushed into the ICU due to respiratory distress, following which she was promptly put on mechanical ventilation. Her delivered oxygen fell to 51\% FiO\textsubscript{2} ...\\
Question: What is the patient's Sequential Organ Failure Assessment (SOFA) Score?}
\end{examplebox}
\vspace{1em}

\noindent\textbf{Prompt:} \\
\noindent The prompt defines the specific computational task the model must perform based on the patient note and the question.

\begin{examplebox}
\noindent\textit{Example:} \\
\noindent\texttt{Given a patient note and a clinical question, compute the requested medical value.} \\
\texttt{<context>} \\
\texttt{Answer only the requested quantity without units. No explanation needed:}
\end{examplebox}


\vspace{1em}

\noindent\textbf{Evaluation Metric:} \\
\noindent\textit{MedCalc Accuracy:} Exact match for discrete categories (e.g., risk, severity, diagnosis); range-based comparison for continuous variables.

\vspace{1em}

\noindent\textbf{Gold Standard Response:} \\
\noindent The gold standard is the correct reference value for the given input. It excludes units and explanation.

\begin{examplebox}
\noindent\textit{Example:} \\
\noindent\texttt{9}
\end{examplebox}

\subsection{CLEAR}

\noindent\textbf{Description:} \\
\noindent CLEAR is a benchmark designed to evaluate models on their ability to detect medical conditions from patient notes using categorical responses. Each instance consists of a clinical note and a target condition, requiring the model to classify the patient's history as either affirmative, negative, or uncertain.

\vspace{1em}

\noindent\textbf{Category:} Clinical Decision Support \\
\noindent\textbf{Subcategory:} Supporting Diagnostic Decisions

\vspace{1em}

\noindent\textbf{Context:} \\
\noindent The context consists of a clinical note documenting patient history, from which the presence or absence of a specific medical condition must be inferred.

\begin{examplebox}
\noindent\textit{Example:} \\
\noindent\texttt{History reviewed. No pertinent family history.\\
Social History\\
Occupational history: Not on file.\\
Social history main topics:\\
\\
Smoking status:\\
...}
\end{examplebox}

\vspace{1em}

\noindent\textbf{Prompt:} \\
\noindent The prompt defines the task of inferring the patient’s medical condition history from a clinical note. The model must choose one of three categorical answers: A (Yes), B (No), or C (Uncertain).

\begin{examplebox}
\noindent\textit{Example:} \\
\noindent\texttt{You are a medical assistant reviewing patient notes. Determine whether the patient has a history of suicidal behavior.\\
\\
Original Clinical Note:\\
<context>\\
\\
Answer:\\
A. Has a history of suicidal behavior\\
B. Does not have a history of suicidal behavior\\
C. Uncertain\\
\\
Respond only with 'A', 'B', or 'C'. Do not add any other text, punctuation, or symbols:}
\end{examplebox}

\vspace{1em}

\noindent\textbf{Evaluation Metric:} \\
\noindent\textit{Exact Match:} The model's response must exactly match the gold standard label (A, B, or C) for the given condition and clinical note.

\vspace{1em}

\noindent\textbf{Gold Standard Response:} \\
\noindent The gold standard represents the correct classification of the patient's condition status for the task.

\begin{examplebox}
\noindent\textit{Example:} \\
\noindent\texttt{B}
\end{examplebox}

\subsection{MTSamples Replicate}

\noindent\textbf{Description:} \\
\noindent MTSamples Replicate is a benchmark that provides transcribed medical reports from various specialties. It is used to evaluate a model’s ability to generate clinically appropriate treatment plans based on unstructured patient documentation.

\vspace{1em}

\noindent\textbf{Category:} Clinical Decision Support \\
\noindent\textbf{Subcategory:} Planning Treatments

\vspace{1em}

\noindent\textbf{Context:} \\
\noindent The context is a medical transcription note documenting the patient's clinical history and current assessment.

\begin{examplebox}
\noindent\textit{Example:} \\
\noindent\texttt{Medical Specialty: Pediatrics - Neonatal\\
Sample Name: 1-year-old Exam - H\&P\\
Description: Health maintenance exam for 1-year-old female.\\
(Medical Transcription Sample Report)\\
...}
\end{examplebox}

\vspace{1em}

\noindent\textbf{Prompt:} \\
\noindent The prompt instructs the model to generate a treatment plan based on the information presented in the patient note. The treatment plan should reflect reasonable clinical judgment and may include diagnostics, prescriptions, or follow-up recommendations.

\begin{examplebox}
\noindent\textit{Example:} \\
\noindent\texttt{Given various information about a patient, return a reasonable treatment plan for the patient.\\
\\
<context>\\
\\
Answer:}
\end{examplebox}

\vspace{1em}

\noindent\textbf{Evaluation Metric:} \\
\noindent\textit{Jury score:} The quality of the model’s answer is evaluated by an LLM-based jury, which assigns an average score based on accuracy, completeness, and clarity with respect to the gold standard response.

\vspace{1em}

\noindent\textbf{Gold Standard Response:} \\
\noindent The gold standard provides a clinically sound treatment plan that serves as a reference for evaluation.

\begin{examplebox}
\noindent\textit{Example:} \\
\noindent\texttt{Diagnostic \& Lab Orders: Ordered blood lead.}
\end{examplebox}

\subsection{Medec}

\noindent\textbf{Description:} \\
\noindent Medec is a benchmark composed of clinical narratives that include either correct documentation or medical errors. Each entry includes sentence-level identifiers and an associated correction task. The model must review the narrative and either identify the erroneous sentence and correct it, or confirm that the text is entirely accurate.

\vspace{1em}

\noindent\textbf{Category:} Clinical Decision Support \\
\noindent\textbf{Subcategory:} Planning Treatments

\vspace{1em}

\noindent\textbf{Context:} \\
\noindent The context is a patient clinical note presented as a numbered list of sentences. Each sentence is preceded by its sentence ID and space, requiring the model to analyze each line independently for correctness.

\begin{examplebox}
\noindent\textit{Example:} \\
\noindent\texttt{0 A 29-year-old internal medicine resident presents to the emergency department with complaints of fevers, diarrhea, abdominal pain, and skin rash for 2 days.\\
1 He feels fatigued and has lost his appetite.\\
...}
\end{examplebox}

\vspace{1em}

\noindent\textbf{Prompt:} \\
\noindent The prompt instructs the model to serve as a medical reviewer of the provided narrative. If the text is correct, the model should return `CORRECT`. If a medical error exists, it must return the line number and a corrected version of the sentence.

\begin{examplebox}
\noindent\textit{Example:} \\
\noindent\texttt{The following is a medical narrative about a patient. You are a skilled medical doctor reviewing the clinical text. The text is either correct or contains one error. The text has a sentence per line. Each line starts with the sentence ID, followed by a space character then the sentence to check. Check every sentence of the text. If the text is correct return the following output: CORRECT. If the text has a medical error, return the sentence ID of the sentence containing the error, followed by a space, and a corrected version of the sentence.\\
\\
Clinical note:\\
<context>\\
\\
Answer:}
\end{examplebox}

\vspace{1em}

\noindent\textbf{Evaluation Metric:} \\
\noindent\textit{Medical Error Flag Accuracy:} Measures whether the model correctly identifies if a note contains an error or not, treating the task as binary classification (correct/incorrect).

\vspace{1em}

\noindent\textbf{Gold Standard Response:} \\
\noindent The gold standard response is either a correction of a specific sentence or the word `CORRECT`, indicating no error was found in the narrative.

\begin{examplebox}
\noindent\textit{Example:} \\
\noindent\texttt{CORRECT}
\end{examplebox}

\subsection{EHRSHOT}

\noindent\textbf{Description:} \\
\noindent EHRSHOT is a benchmark designed to evaluate a model’s ability to predict future clinical events using structured EHR data. Each instance contains a patient's historical EHR data and a forward-looking clinical question about whether a particular diagnosis, lab result, or hospital event will occur.

\vspace{1em}

\noindent\textbf{Category:} Clinical Decision Support \\
\noindent\textbf{Subcategory:} Predicting Patient Risks and Outcomes

\vspace{1em}

\noindent\textbf{Context:} \\
\noindent The context includes the patient's EHR history, represented as structured clinical codes. The model must determine whether a future clinical event, such as a diagnosis, lab abnormality, or hospital outcome, is likely to occur based on this history.

\begin{examplebox}
\noindent\textit{Example:} \\
\noindent\texttt{\# Instructions\\
You are an expert hospitalist at Stanford Healthcare, an academic medical center affiliated with Stanford University. You specialize in predicting 30-day readmission.\\
\\
Instruction: Review the patient's EHR history. Based on all available medical evidence in the provided EHR, please answer the question: If this patient is discharged from the hospital right now, is the patient likely to be readmitted to the hospital within 30 days?\\
\\
Patient EHR:\\
...}
\end{examplebox}

\vspace{1em}

\noindent\textbf{Prompt:} \\
\noindent The prompt requests a binary prediction from the model based on the EHR context. The response must strictly follow the format: ‘A’ for yes, ‘B’ for no, with no additional text.

\begin{examplebox}
\noindent\textit{Example:} \\
\noindent\texttt{Answer A for yes, B for no.\\
\\
<context>\\
\\
A. yes\\
B. no\\
Respond with only 'A' for yes or 'B' for no. Do not add any other text, punctuation, or symbols:}
\end{examplebox}

\vspace{1em}

\noindent\textbf{Evaluation Metric:} \\
\noindent\textit{Exact Match:} The predicted answer is compared directly to the gold standard label. The response must exactly match either 'A' or 'B'.

\vspace{1em}

\noindent\textbf{Gold Standard Response:} \\
\noindent The correct answer to the predictive question. It is represented as one of two options: A (yes) or B (no).

\begin{examplebox}
\noindent\textit{Example:} \\
\noindent\texttt{A}
\end{examplebox}

\subsection{HeadQA}

\noindent\textbf{Description:} \\
\noindent HeadQA is a benchmark consisting of biomedical multiple-choice questions intended to evaluate a model’s medical knowledge and reasoning. Each instance presents a clinical or scientific question with four answer options, requiring the model to select the most appropriate answer.

\vspace{1em}

\noindent\textbf{Category:} Clinical Decision Support \\
\noindent\textbf{Subcategory:} Providing Clinical Knowledge Support

\vspace{1em}

\noindent\textbf{Context:} \\
\noindent The context consists of a multiple-choice question in the medical domain. The model must review the question and answer choices, then select the most appropriate clinical response.

\begin{examplebox}
\noindent\textit{Example:} \\
\noindent\texttt{In the stools of a 6-year-old boy with diarrhea and abdominal pain, a packet of parasite eggs surrounded by a transparent membrane is observed. Which treatment do you consider most appropriate?\\
\\
A. Praziquantel\\
B. Azithromycin\\
C. Metronidazole\\
D. Albendazole}
\end{examplebox}

\vspace{1em}

\noindent\textbf{Prompt:} \\
\noindent The prompt directs the model to answer biomedical multiple-choice questions by selecting the correct option. The model is expected to return only the letter corresponding to its answer.

\begin{examplebox}
\noindent\textit{Example:} \\
\noindent\texttt{You are a highly knowledgeable AI assistant specializing in biomedical sciences. Your task is to answer multiple-choice questions accurately based on the options provided. Each question will relate to biomedical concepts, and you will be asked to choose the most appropriate answer.\\
\\
Select the correct answer by outputting only the letter corresponding to your choice (A, B, C, or D).\\
\\
Question:\\
<context>\\
\\
Answer:}
\end{examplebox}

\vspace{1em}

\noindent\textbf{Evaluation Metric:} \\
\noindent\textit{Exact Match:} The model's answer must exactly match the gold standard letter label corresponding to the correct choice (A, B, C, or D).

\vspace{1em}

\noindent\textbf{Gold Standard Response:} \\
\noindent The correct answer to the question, given as a single letter corresponding to the appropriate choice.

\begin{examplebox}
\noindent\textit{Example:} \\
\noindent\texttt{A}
\end{examplebox}

\noindent\textbf{Category:} Clinical Decision Support \\
\noindent\textbf{Subcategory:} Providing Clinical Knowledge Support

\vspace{1em}

\noindent\textbf{Context:} \\
\noindent The context consists of structured EHR data encoded as an event stream, along with a natural language question or instruction referencing some aspect of the patient’s medical record. The model must accurately extract and synthesize relevant information.

\begin{examplebox}
\noindent\textit{Example:} \\
\noindent\texttt{EHR: <eventstream person\_id="xxxxxxxxxx">\\
<encounter start\_timestamp="xxxx-xx-xx xx:xx" end\_timestamp="xxxx-xx-xx xx:xx">\\
\ \ \ <person>\\
\ \ \ \ \ <birthdate>xxxx-xx-xx</birthdate>\\
\ \ \ \ \ <age>\\
\ \ \ \ \ \ \ <days>xx</days>\\
\ \ \ \ \ \ \ <years>xx</years>\\
\ \ \ \ \ </age>\\
\ \ \ \ \ <demographics>\\
\ \ \ \ \ \ \ <ethnicity>xxxxxxxxxxx</ethnicity>\\
\ \ \ \ \ \ \ <gender>xxxxxxxxxxx</gender>\\
\ \ \ \ \ </demographics>\\
\ \ \ \ \ <payerplan>xxxxx</payerplan>\\
\ \ \ </person>\\
\ \ \ <events>\\
...\\
Question: Please show the patient's vital signs over the past 3 months.}
\end{examplebox}

\vspace{1em}

\noindent\textbf{Prompt:} \\
\noindent The prompt gives an instruction and asks the model to fulfill it using the EHR context provided. The response should be clinically relevant, accurate, and formatted appropriately for the instruction.

\begin{examplebox}
\noindent\textit{Example:} \\
\noindent\texttt{Instruction: Answer the following question based on the EHR:\\
\\
<context>\\
\\
Answer:}
\end{examplebox}

\vspace{1em}

\noindent\textbf{Evaluation Metric:} \\

\vspace{1em}

\noindent\textbf{Gold Standard Response:} \\
\noindent The gold response is a human-written answer by a clinician addressing the instruction based on the patient record.

\begin{examplebox}
\noindent\textit{Example:} \\

- xx/xx/xxxx: Weight: xxxxx Height: xxxxx Head Circumference: xxxxx Temperature: xxxxx\\
- xx/xx/xxxx: Pulse: xxxxx Temp: xxxxx Weight: xxxxx Height: xxxxxx\\
